Oasis - A Student Portal for Medical School

At the WashU Medical School, students are required to document their patient encounters through the student portal. While the data-entry task is supposed to be simple, the faculty has found that a majority of students find it overburdening, resulting in inaccurate reports.

Stakeholder wishes to create a digital experience that encourages responsible data entry for very busy medical students

Why do medical schools require students to enter patient log in the first place?

Documentation is an essential and important feature of patient care and learning how and what to document is an important part of medical education. Keeping this log becomes a student training exercise in documentation. All of these features of documentation - seriousness, accuracy, conscientiousness and honesty - are measures of professionalism. ––– TAMU Medical School

Do the medical students understand the rational behind this mandatory documentation?

“Annoying busy work. It’s purely administrative, and just for attendance purpose. The whole process could potentially just be automated through the EMR system.”

One interesting we found during our interview was that Comparing to digital devices, student stick to paper as their main medium during patient visits. sometimes they would use a piece of scrap paper to write down patient information and they lose those paper. As a result, making up faulty patient information when entering into the system Rather than introducing a new medium such as a new app on their phone, we wanted to stick to what the students are used to the most- pen and paper. We decided to design a notebook specifically for these busy students
